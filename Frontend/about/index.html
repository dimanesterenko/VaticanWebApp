History

Formation

The Vatican Museums trace their origin to a single marble sculpture, purchased in the 16th century: Laocoön and His Sons was discovered on 14 January 1506, in a vineyard near the basilica of Santa Maria Maggiore in Rome.

Art galleries

The Vatican Pinacoteca is a collection of paintings. The collection includes such famous works as “Transfiguration”, “Coronation of Mary” by Raphael; "Saint Jerome" by Leonardo da Vinci.

Treasures Unveiled

We display works from the immense collection amassed by the Catholic Church and the papacy throughout the centuries, including several of the most well-known Roman sculptures and most important masterpieces of Renaissance art in the world.

Main Purposes

Preservation of Cultural Heritage

To safeguard and preserve a diverse range of cultural and artistic treasures.

Cultural Exchange

To facilitate cultural exchange by collaborating with other museums and institutions worldwide.

Promotion of Art and Culture

To showcase and promote artistic achievements, particularly emphasizing the Renaissance masterpieces, Roman sculptures.

Inspiration

To serve as a source of inspiration, allowing visitors to appreciate the beauty, creativity, and diversity of human expression through art.

Information Dissemination

To provide accurate and up-to-date information about the collections, exhibitions, events, and activities taking place within the Vatican Museums.

Visitor Guidance

To offer practical information for visitors, including details on opening hours, ticketing, guided tours, and other visitor services.

Digital Publications

To embrace digital technologies to enhance the virtual museum experience, allowing for new ways to engage with the collections online.

Feedback and Engagement

To get better by staying in touch.
